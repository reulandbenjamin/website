Environmental Measurement Station

Automated climate data collection for scientific analysis

Context

As part of my electronics studies at HEPL, I developed an autonomous measurement station capable of collecting environmental data (temperature, humidity, pressure, air quality) for research applications.

Problem

Commercial weather stations are expensive and inflexible. The goal was to create an open-source, modular, and economical solution for continuous measurements.

Solution

Using a Raspberry Pi Zero W coupled with an Enviro+ HAT (Pimoroni) integrating 7 sensors: BME280, LTR-559, MICS6814, PMS5003.

Tech stack: Python 3, enviroplus library, CSV storage, Matplotlib visualizations.

Impact

  • Data collected every 10 minutes for 3 months
  • Total cost: ~€60 (vs €300+ for commercial solutions)
  • Project reused by 2 fellow students

Technologies

Python 3 Raspberry Pi Enviro+ HAT
